# Visual Strategy: Parent Document Retrieval (Hierarchy)

This guide focuses on creating a "Sculptural Hierarchy" visual that conveys the relationship between small chunks and large documents without looking like a messy flowchart.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Connection & Hierarchy"
**Concept**: A minimalist installation art piece. One large center block (Parent) connected by precise silver threads to several scattered smaller blocks (Children). This represents the structured retrieval path from a small search hit back to its full context.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Minimalist Gallery aesthetic
- Tactile materials (Light oak wood, polished silver)
- Soft, natural museum lighting
- Sophisticated and calm

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional editorial photography of a minimalist modern art installation in a bright gallery. A single large, square block of light oak wood sits in the center. Ten smaller cubes of matching light oak are scattered around it on a clean white surface. Each smaller cube is connected to the large center block by a single, thin, high-tension polished silver wire. Soft, diffused natural light from a high window creates long, gentle shadows. Neutral color palette (white, light wood, silver). High-end aesthetic, shallow depth of field, sharp focus on the central block and its connections. 8k resolution, photorealistic textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: Setting the Scene**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Airy" and "Clean" space.

**Step 2: Refining the "Human Touch"**
If the image looks too generated/perfect, ask Gemini:
> *“Keep the architecture but make the wood grain more visible and realistic. The silver wires should have subtle glints of light only where they catch the sun. Ensure the shadows are soft and have a slight blur towards the edges to mimic real daylight.”*

**Step 3: Vertical Composition (Optional)**
If you want to emphasize the "Parent" as a higher authority, try:
> *“Place the large block on a slightly higher minimalist pedestal, with the smaller blocks sitting on the gallery floor, maintaining the silver wire connections. This creates a vertical sense of retrieval hierarchy.”*
