# Visual Strategy: Whisper & Diarization (The Voiceprints)

This guide shows how to create an "Identity from Sound" visual representing speaker diarization and voice identification using high-end topographical and light themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Distinctive Voices"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of "Sonic Identity." Two separate, glowing soundwave 3D shapes (Voiceprints) are captured in a dark charcoal space. One voiceprint glows with a warm Amber light, and the other with a vibrant Cyan light. They look like complex, jagged topographical maps. A single, razor-sharp glowing white line (The Identifier) is analyzing the differences between the two.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Scientific and forensic
- High-precision data analysis
- Moody and technical
- Contrast between "Warm Amber" and "Clinical Cyan"

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional archaeological-style photography in a dark, minimalist studio. Two distinct 3D topographical structures made of light are resting on a dark charcoal platform. One structure glows with an intense, warm amber light; the other glows with a vibrant, electric cyan light. Each structure represents a unique 'voiceprint' with jagged peaks and valleys. A thin, perfectly horizontal bar of glowing white light is positioned between them, as if comparing their frequencies. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the textures of the amber and cyan light structures. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.0. 8k resolution, photorealistic light and stone textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Identity**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Unique Human Signature."

**Step 2: Refining the "Jagged" detail**
If the voiceprints look like simple smooth waves, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the amber and cyan structures more complex and jagged. They should look like highly detailed 3D maps of a voice's frequency spectrum. Each peak should be sharp, representing a specific sonic characteristic that the AI is using to distinguish the speaker.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Comparison"**
If the white light bar is too thick, ask for precision:
> *“The central white light bar should be as thin as a razor—representing the extreme precision of the diarization algorithm. The background should be a near-total black abyss, making the glowing voiceprints the only reality in the frame.”*
