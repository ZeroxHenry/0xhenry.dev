# Visual Strategy: Supervisor Pattern (The Authority)

This guide shows how to create a "Hierarchical Control" visual representing management and delegation using minimalist architectural and geometric themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Leadership & Delegation"
**Concept**: A studio product photograph of a geometric hierarchy. A single, large, sharp-edged pearl-white pyramid (The Supervisor) is positioned prominently in a dark charcoal space. Below it, in a structured grid, are four smaller, neutral grey spheres (The Specialists). Glowing white filaments originate from the tip of the pyramid and connect to each sphere, showing centralized command.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Authoritative and organized
- Precise and hierarchical
- Minimalist and professional
- High-end architectural feel

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional editorial photography of a minimalist geometric arrangement. A single large, sharp pearl-white porcelain pyramid stands at the center of a dark charcoal platform. Arranged in a perfect lower-tier grid around it are four smaller matte grey stone spheres. Thin, sharp glowing white filaments connect the peak of the pyramid to each of the spheres. Cinematic top-down lighting creates long, clean shadows. Deep bokeh, sharp focus on the pyramid's peak and the light connections. No text, no artifacts, premium tech aesthetic. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.8. 8k resolution, photorealistic porcelain and stone textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Command**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Structure and Order."

**Step 2: Refining the "Authority"**
If the pyramid looks too plain, tell Gemini:
> *“Make the white porcelain material of the pyramid look more premium—give it a subtle, soft internal luminescence. The connecting filaments should look like high-tension, razor-sharp white light, emphasizing the strength of the coordination.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Shadows**
If the scene is too flat, guide it:
> *“Increase the contrast of the lighting. The shadows cast by the pyramid and spheres should be deep and sharp, defining the three-dimensional space of the hierarchy. The background should be a near-black, clean charcoal to make the white and grey subjects stand out.”*
