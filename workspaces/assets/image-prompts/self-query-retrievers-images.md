# Visual Strategy: Self-Querying Retrievers (The Curator)

This guide shows how to create a "Selective Filtering" visual using advanced lighting and robotics themes, maintaining a clean and professional look.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Automated Selection"
**Concept**: A sleek, high-end laboratory shot. A minimalist white robotic arm is holding a precise circular glass lens over a dark surface covered in various components (representing data). The lens highlights only one specific, glowing part while the rest stay in shadow. This represents the AI "choosing" the right metadata filters.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Futuristic but grounded
- Minimalist and clean
- Technical precision
- High contrast (Spotlight effect)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional product photography in a minimalist laboratory. A sleek, pearl-white robotic arm with smooth joints is holding a high-precision optical magnifying glass over a dark, matte charcoal table. On the table are various small, grey mechanical parts. The magnifying glass is centered over a single part that is glowing with a clean, soft white light. The view through the lens is crystal clear and sharp, while the surroundings are in a deep focus blur (bokeh). Soft, dramatic top-down studio lighting. No text, no messy cables, premium tech aesthetic. Shot on 85mm lens, f/1.8. 8k resolution, photorealistic."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Initial Filter**
Paste the prompt. Focus on the "Robotic Arm" and "Lens" interaction.

**Step 2: Refining the Robotics**
If the robot looks too "sci-fi" or scary, tell Gemini:
> *“Make the robotic arm look more like a piece of high-end medical or laboratory equipment—clean, white, and elegant. Remove any visible wires or gritty textures. It should look like a premium consumer electronic product.”*

**Step 3: Perfecting the Highlight**
If the highlight isn't strong enough, guide it:
> *“Make the contrast between the single highlighted part and the rest of the table stronger. The rest of the objects should be almost silhouettes, while the part under the lens should be the clear and vibrant hero of the image.”*
