# Visual Strategy: Automated Refactoring (The Refiner)

This guide shows how to create a "Purification and Alignment" visual representing tech debt removal and code refactoring using high-end stone and light themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Polishing the Legacy"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of "The Transformation." A single, large slab of raw black volcanic obsidian (The Legacy Code) is suspended in a dark charcoal space. One half of the slab is rough, jagged, and cracked. A vertical plane of razor-sharp white light (The Refactoring Agent) is slowly passing over the slab. Behind the light plane, the obsidian is becoming perfectly smooth, mirror-polished, and inscribed with clean, glowing cyan lines of code.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Transformative and purifying
- Precise and artisanal
- High-end industrial design aesthetic
- Contrast between "Rough Stone" and "Mirror Finish"

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional artistic photography in a dark, minimalist studio. A large, horizontal slab of black obsidian is captured mid-transformation. The left side is raw, jagged, and cracked. A vertical 'wall' of intense white light is moving from right to left across the slab. On the right side of the light wall, the obsidian has been transformed into a perfectly smooth, mirror-polished surface with thin, orderly glowing cyan lines etched into it. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the transition line where the white light touches the stone. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.8. 8k resolution, photorealistic stone and light textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Polish**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Chaos becoming Order."

**Step 2: Refining the "Legacy" texture**
If the rough side of the stone looks too uniform, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the raw side of the obsidian look more 'entangled' and messy, with deep cracks and uneven surfaces, representing the complexity of technical debt. The light wall should look like a laser-thin surgical instrument that is physically carving away the mess.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Reflections"**
If the smooth side looks like simple plastic, ask for realism:
> *“The polished side of the stone should have the internal depth of a high-end luxury watch face. Ensure we can see the reflections of the glowing cyan code lines appearing deep within the glass-like surface of the obsidian. The overall environment should remain pitch black to emphasize the transformation.”*
