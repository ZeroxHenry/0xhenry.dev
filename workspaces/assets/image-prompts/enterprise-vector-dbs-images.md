# Visual Strategy: Weaviate & Milvus (Enterprise Vector DBs)

This document outlines the visual vision and step-by-step Gemini instructions to create a premium, non-AI aesthetic for this technical post.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "The Trillion-Scale Network"
**Concept**: A sleek, macro photography shot of crystalline data nodes glowing subtly in a vast, dark network. This represents the scalability and high-density storage of enterprise databases like Milvus.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Minimalist
- High-end corporate tech
- Professional studio photography
- Realistic textures (glass, light refractions)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional macro photography of a structured network made of translucent, interconnected crystalline cubes. Each cube has a soft, internal teal glow. The background is a vast, pitch-black void with a subtle hint of charcoal gray gradients. Focus is sharp on the central cube with a deep bokeh effect in the foreground and background. Soft studio side lighting, high-end product photography style, 100mm macro lens, f/2.8. No text, no cartoonish elements, realistic glass textures, 8k resolution."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Initial Roll**
Paste the prompt above into Gemini. Gemini should produce a clean, minimalist image.

**Step 2: Refining the "AI Look" (Optional)**
If the result looks too "glowy" or saturated (common AI behavior), give Gemini this follow-up feedback:
> *“Keep the composition exactly the same, but reduce the saturation. Make the glow more subtle, like real LED indicators behind frosted glass. Increase the film grain slightly for a more organic, photographic feel.”*

**Step 3: Adjusting for Text Placement**
If you need to add your own title text later, ask Gemini to move the subject to one side:
> *“Shift the main crystalline structure to the right third of the frame, leaving the left side as a clean, dark negative space for text overlays.”*
