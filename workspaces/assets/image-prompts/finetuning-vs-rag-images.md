# Visual Strategy: Fine-tuning vs. RAG (Internal vs. External)

This guide shows how to create a "Location of Knowledge" visual representing internal training vs. external retrieval using neural and architectural themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "The Core vs. The Collection"
**Concept**: A balanced, split-screen composition.
- **Left Side (Fine-tuning)**: A high-end macro shot of a complex, glowing white neural network structure (The Brain). It represents knowledge embedded directly into the machine's weights.
- **Right Side (RAG)**: A minimalist, dark charcoal shelf containing several perfectly arranged glass books that emit a soft cyan glow (The Library). It represents knowledge retrieved from an external source.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Scientific and structural
- Contrast between "Organic/Connected" and "Modular/Retrieved"
- Both sides unified by a dark, premium charcoal atmosphere
- High-end material luxury (Glow-white filaments vs. Cyan glass)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional dual-composition photography on a dark charcoal background. On the left side, a macro shot of a complex, interconnected fiber-optic neural network glowing with a pure pearl-white light. On the right side, a single minimalist charcoal shelf holds three upright, polished cyan-glass books that glow softly from within. The composition is balanced and clean. Deep bokeh on both backgrounds, sharp focus on the central neural node and the central glass book. No text, no artifacts, premium tech aesthetic. Shot on 50mm primary lens. 8k resolution, photorealistic light interaction and glass textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Comparison**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a clear visual distinction between "Training" and "Retrieving."

**Step 2: Refining the "Neural" look**
If the left side looks like messy wires, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the neural network on the left more organized and architectural, like a high-end fiber optic installation. The connections should be razor-sharp and the glow should feel like concentrated data, not just light.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Glass Glow**
If the books on the right look like plastic blocks, ask for realism:
> *“Enhance the glass texture of the books. They should look like heavy, high-clarity optical glass with polished edges. The cyan glow should come from deep inside the material, refracting realistically at the edges of the book spine.”*
