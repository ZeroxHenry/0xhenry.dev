# Visual Strategy: Handling PDF Tables (The Grid)

This guide shows how to create a "Structured Grid" visual that represents organized data extraction, using minimalist architectural photography styles.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Structure from Chaos"
**Concept**: A macro photograph of a minimalist architectural model. Thin, precise white lines form a perfect grid over alternating layers of clear glass and brushed aluminum. This represents the "Table Structure" being identified and extracted from the "Chaos" of a PDF.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Architectural and structured
- Minimalist and clean
- Technical precision
- Neutral tones (White, silver, glass)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional architectural macro photography. A minimalist abstract structure made of stacked layers of clear glass and polished white aluminum. A thin, perfect glowing white grid is projected onto the glass surface, representing data cells. Shallow depth of field, sharp focus on the central intersection of the grid lines. High-end museum lighting, clean shadows, no artifacts, no text. Realistic glass reflections and metallic textures. Shot on 85mm lens, f/2.8. 8k resolution, photorealistic."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Layer**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for that sense of "Math organized in physical space."

**Step 2: Refining the "Technical" Look**
If it looks too much like a "building," tell Gemini:
> *“Make it more abstract. It shouldn't look like a real building, but like a conceptual model of a database grid. Make the lines thinner and sharper, like laser projections on glass. The atmosphere should be very clean and silent.”*

**Step 3: Color Temperature**
If you want a cooler, more "software" feel, ask:
> *“Cool down the white balance slightly to give it a faint blue/cyan tint. This will make the glass and metal feel more like a high-tech lab environment.”*
