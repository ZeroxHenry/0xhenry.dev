# Visual Strategy: Repository Context (The Neural Map)

This guide shows how to create an "Intelligent Navigation" visual representing code indexing and repository-level awareness using high-end networking and light themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "The Connected Codebase"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of "The Knowledge Graph." A dense, highly complex web of razor-thin glowing cyan light filaments is suspended in a dark charcoal space. Each intersection of filaments represents a "Symbol" or "File." A single, intense beam of white light (The Contextual Query) is traveling through the web, illuminating a direct path between four distant points, symbolizing the AI's ability to connect information across different parts of the project.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Connected and complex
- Rapid and efficient
- High-end laboratory data visualization
- Contrast between "Clinical White" and "Deep Electric Cyan"

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional artistic photography in a dark, minimalist studio. A dense, intricate web of thousands of razor-thin glowing cyan light filaments is floating in the center of the frame, representing a complex data network. A single, intense pulse of pure white light is captured mid-travel as it flows along a specific path through the network, illuminating everything in its wake. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the white light pulse and the illuminated path. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 85mm lens, f/1.4. 8k resolution, photorealistic light and fiber-optic textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Fast Path**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Global Understanding."

**Step 2: Refining the "Filament" density**
If the web looks too sparse, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the cyan network much denser—it should look like a complex 3D nervous system or a fiber-optic brain. The white light pulse should feel powerful, as if it is successfully finding a needle in a haystack of code.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Glow" diffusion**
If the cyan light is too blurry, ask for sharpness:
> *“Each cyan filament should be as sharp as a laser. The white light's brightness should realistically reflect off neighboring cyan lines as it passes them, creating a sense of three-dimensional space and physical presence within the dark abyss.”*
