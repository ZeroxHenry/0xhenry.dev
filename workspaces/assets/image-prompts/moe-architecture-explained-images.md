# Visual Strategy: Mixture of Experts (The Hive)

This guide shows how to create an "Internal Specialization" visual representing MoE architecture using high-end geometric crystal and light themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Selective Intelligence"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of a "Crystalline Grid." A large, complex geometric crystal structure (resembling a cross between a brain and a computer chip) floats in a dark void. Inside the crystal are dozens of small cubic chambers (The Experts). Only two or three of these chambers are glowing intensely with a sharp cyan or gold light, showing that they are the only "Experts" activated for the current task.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Complex and structured
- Selective and efficient
- High-end jewelry and science aesthetic
- Precise and multicolored light interaction

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional macro photography of a complex, multi-faceted geometric crystal sculpture in a dark charcoal studio. The crystal is semi-translucent and filled with a grid of smaller internal chambers. Most chambers are dim, but three specific chambers glow intensely—one with vibrant cyan light, one with warm golden light, and one with pure white light. The light refracts beautifully through the facets of the outer crystal. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the glowing internal chambers. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.0. 8k resolution, photorealistic crystal and light textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Collective Activation**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Hidden Specialization within a larger Structure."

**Step 2: Refining the "Facet" detail**
If the crystal looks like a single blurry blob, guide Gemini:
> *“Keep the composition but sharpen the edges of the outer crystal facets. I want to see the microscopic scratches and the perfect clarity of the glass. The internal chambers should be clearly defined cubes, almost like a 3D CPU architecture made of light.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Colors**
If the colors are too muddy or mixed, ask for separation:
> *“Ensure the cyan, gold, and white lights are distinct and don't bleed into each other too much. They should look like laser-focused experts, each handling a different part of the problem. The surrounding dim chambers should be a dark, translucent grey, showing they are inactive.”*
