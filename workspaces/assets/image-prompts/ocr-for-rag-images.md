# Visual Strategy: OCR for RAG (The Scan)

This guide shows how to create a "Digital Metamorphosis" visual representing OCR—converting physical paper into digital intelligence.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Illuminating the Invisible"
**Concept**: A dark, moody studio shot of a stack of slightly aged paper documents. A thin, high-intensity cyan laser line is sweeping across the page. Where the laser hits the text, the characters glow brightly and appear to "lift" off the paper as digital pixels.

**Target Vibe**: 
- High-tech and sophisticated
- Cinematic lighting (Neon vs. Shadow)
- Minimalist and mysterious
- Professional tech lab aesthetic

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional cinematic photography. A close-up shot of a stack of clean white papers in a pitch-black studio. A single, razor-sharp cyan laser line is projected horizontally across the top page. Where the laser line touches the paper, the letters underneath glow with an intense, clean cyan light and small digital square particles are rising from the paper. Shallow depth of field, sharp focus on the laser's interaction with the text. Soft ambient blue light, high-end tech aesthetic, no artifacts, no text legibility required. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.0. 8k resolution, photorealistic."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Initial Scan**
Submit the core prompt. You want a "laser-sharp" interaction point and a very dark environment.

**Step 2: Refining the "Digital" effect**
If the "rising particles" look like generic sparkles, tell Gemini:
> *“Make the rising particles look like precise, tiny digital cubes or data bits, not magic sparkles. They should feel like they are being extracted by the laser. The cyan light should cast a realistic glow on the surrounding paper fibers.”*

**Step 3: Texture Balance**
If the paper looks too computerized, guide it:
> *“Enhance the texture of the paper—I want to see the subtle pulp and grain of a real sheet of high-quality paper. This contrast between the organic paper and the high-tech laser will make the image feel much more premium.”*
