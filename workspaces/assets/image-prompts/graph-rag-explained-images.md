# Visual Strategy: GraphRAG (The Constellation)

This guide shows how to create a "Relational Intelligence" visual representing Knowledge Graphs using minimalist astronomical and network aesthetics.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Connection & Insight"
**Concept**: A high-end 3D visual of a complex neural-like network in dark space. Dozens of small, glowing golden nodes are connected by thin, sharp white light filaments. Certain clusters of nodes are closer together and glow with a collective intensity, representing "Graph Communities" and aggregate knowledge.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Futuristic and airy
- Minimalist connectivity
- High-end scientific data visualization
- Cinematic depth (Bokeh)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional cinematic 3D visualization. A network of hundreds of tiny, glowing golden nodes suspended in a deep charcoal 3D space. The nodes are interconnected by extremely thin, sharp filaments of pure white light. Some nodes are grouped into tight, brighter-glowing clusters. The composition is dynamic, with a strong sense of depth and perspective. Shallow depth of field, with nodes in the far background and extreme foreground artistically blurred. No text, no artifacts, premium tech aesthetic. Shot on 50mm primary lens. 8k resolution, photorealistic light interaction."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Connection**
Submit the initial prompt. Look for a balance between the golden nodes and the white links.

**Step 2: Refining the "Organic" feel**
If the network looks too stiff or like a regular grid, tell Gemini:
> *“Make the network distribution more organic and natural, like a constellation or a complex neural network. Ensure there are varied distances between nodes, with some dense 'high-intelligence' clusters and some sparse connections.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Light**
If the light is too overwhelming (too bright/white), ask for more subtlety:
> *“Reduce the thickness of the white links so they are as thin as spider silk. Increase the intensity of the golden glow of the nodes themselves. The light should feel like it's vibrating with data, but the overall image should remain dark and minimalist.”*
