# Visual Strategy: Local Fine-tuning (The Sculptor)

This guide shows how to create a "Precise Adaptation" visual representing fine-tuning and LoRA using minimalist material contrast (Glass and Stone).

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Refinement of the Core"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of a "Transformation." A large, heavy, matte-grey stone monolith (The Base Model) is being touched by a perfectly clear, high-detail glass hand (The LoRA Adapter). Only at the points where the glass touches the stone are there glowing white lines of energy, showing that the adjustment is targeted and highly precise.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Artistic and metaphorical
- Technical precision
- Contrast between "Weight" and "Clarity"
- High-end museum installation aesthetic

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional artistic photography in a dark, minimalist studio. A large, upright matte charcoal stone monolith with a rough natural texture is the centerpiece. A life-sized, perfectly transparent glass hand is delicately touching the surface of the stone. At the exact points of contact, razor-sharp glowing white lines of light sprawl across the stone's surface like neural pathways. Cinematic side lighting, deep shadows, high-end conceptual art aesthetic. Deep bokeh, sharp focus on the glass fingertips and the glowing light. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.8. 8k resolution, photorealistic glass and stone textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Adaptation**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Delicate Power."

**Step 2: Refining the "Glass" hand**
If the hand looks too ghostly or like a drawing, guide Gemini:
> *“Keep the hand perfectly transparent but ensure it has realistic light refractions and fingerprint-level detail in the glass. It should look like solid, heavy optical glass, not a hologram. The reflections on the glass should catch the glowing white lines from the stone surface.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Stone" scale**
If the stone looks like a small pebble, ask for more scale:
> *“Make the stone monolith look large and imposing, like an ancient monument. The rough texture should contrast sharply with the perfection of the glass hand. The glowing lines should look like they are being etched or written into the stone in real-time by the touch of the glass hand.”*
