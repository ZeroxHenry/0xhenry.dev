# Visual Strategy: Multi-Query Retrieval (The Prism)

This guide shows how to create a "Spectrum" visual that represents expanding a single idea into many, using professional photography principles.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Intent Expansion & Diversity"
**Concept**: A high-end macro shot of a glass prism splitting a single white light beam into a vibrant but controlled spectrum. This represents the LLM taking one user query and re-phrasing it into multiple search queries to cover more ground.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Scientific but artistic
- Sharp focus on the glass edge
- Clean light refraction
- Dark, moody background to emphasize the colors

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "High-end cinematic photography of a sharp-edged crystal prism on a dark, reflective black glass surface. A single, thin beam of bright white light enters from the left and hits the prism, splitting into exactly five distinct, vibrant beams of colored light (cyan, magenta, amber, green, and violet) on the right side. The light beams should be clean and straight, illuminate a dark background with subtle dust motes. Shallow depth of field, sharp focus on the prism's refraction point. Shot on 50mm prime lens, f/2.0. Minimalist, premium tech aesthetic. 8k resolution, photorealistic."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Spectrum Capture**
Input the prompt. You want to see "clean" beams, not messy rainbows. It should feel like a laser experiment.

**Step 2: Cleaning the Colors**
If Gemini makes it look like a "Pride flag" or a messy splash, guide it with:
> *“Make the five beams more distinct and separate. They should look like thin, professional laser beams emerging from the glass, not a blended rainbow. Darken the environment so the light is the only subject.”*

**Step 3: Texture Refinement**
If the glass looks fake, ask:
> *“Enhance the surface reflections on the black glass the prism is sitting on. I want to see a perfect, subtle reflection of the light beams on the floor as well. Ensure the crystal has realistic internal reflections and sharp edges.”*
