# Visual Strategy: AI PR Reviews (The Gatekeeper)

This guide shows how to create a "Scrutiny and Approval" visual representing automated code review and CI/CD gates using high-end industrial and light themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "The Analytical Filter"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of "The Barrier of Quality." A thick, vertical slab of smoke-grey translucent glass (The Review Gate) stands in a dark void. A stream of mixed white and red light particles (The Raw Pull Request) is hitting the front of the glass. On the other side of the glass, only pure, perfectly parallel beams of cyan light (The Approved Code) are emerging. This visualizes the filtration and improvement process of an AI reviewer.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Selective and rigorous
- High-security and industrial
- Clean and atmospheric
- Contrast between "Chaotic Mixed Light" and "Ordered Cyan Beams"

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional industrial photography in a dark, minimalist studio. A thick vertical slab of smoke-grey translucent glass is positioned as a divider. A chaotic cloud of intertwined red and white light particles hits the right side of the glass. On the left side of the glass, beautiful, perfectly parallel beams of vibrant cyan light are emerging in a straight line. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the point where the light penetrates the glass. No text, no artifacts. Shot on 85mm lens, f/1.4. 8k resolution, photorealistic glass and light textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Filter**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Quality through Filtration."

**Step 2: Refining the "Chaos"**
If the red and white light looks too orderly, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the light hitting the right side of the glass look more 'noisy' and disorganized—representing the raw state of a PR before review. The cyan beams on the other side should be so straight and perfect they look like they were carved from crystal, representing 'Production Ready' code.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Glass" depth**
If the glass looks thin, ask for mass:
> *“The smoke-grey glass should look very thick and heavy, like a vault door. Ensure it has realistic internal refractions and that some of the 'bad' red light is visibly trapped and absorbed within the material of the glass itself.”*
