# Visual Strategy: ReAct Pattern (The Thinking Loop)

This guide shows how to create a "Cyclical Intelligence" visual representing the Thinking-Acting loop using minimalist optical physics themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Reasoned Action"
**Concept**: A high-end macro shot of a single triangular glass prism on a dark surface. A thin beam of white light (Thought) enters the prism. It emerges as a sharp, focused beam of cyan light (Action) that hits a small white mirror (Observation). The mirror reflects a soft, diffused glow back towards the prism, completing the loop.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Mathematical and logical
- Clean light refraction
- High-end laboratory aesthetic
- Precise and repeating structure

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional cinematic photography. A dark, minimalist studio with a single high-clarity triangular crystal prism on a reflective black glass surface. A thin, concentrated beam of white light enters the prism from the left side. It refracts into a single, sharp cyan beam that strikes a small, high-polish silver mirror in the background. The mirror reflects a soft white light back toward the prism. Deep bokeh, shallow depth of field, sharp focus on the refraction point inside the crystal. Minimalist, premium tech aesthetic, no text, no artifacts. Shot on 100mm macro lens, f/2.0. 8k resolution, photorealistic light and glass textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The Initial Refraction**
Submit the core prompt. You want a very clean "Laser-like" beam of light and a clear crystal.

**Step 2: Refining the "Loop"**
If the reflection isn't clear, guide Gemini:
> *“Make the reflection from the silver mirror much more evident. It should look like a soft, returning beam of information that completes a cycle with the original white beam. Ensure the prism has realistic internal reflections and sharp, defined edges.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Colors**
If the rainbow effect is too much, ask for simplicity:
> *“Restrict the colors to only pure white and a single concentrated cyan beam. Remove the spectrum rainbow to maintain a more technical, diagnostic look. The shadows should be pitch black to make the light paths the hero of the image.”*
