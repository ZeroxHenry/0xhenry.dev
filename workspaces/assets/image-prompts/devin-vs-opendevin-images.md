# Visual Strategy: Devin vs. OpenDevin (The Locked and The Open)

This guide shows how to create a "Philosophy of Development" visual representing Closed-source vs. Open-source using minimalist geometric and material themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Different Paths, Same Goal"
**Concept**: A balanced, split-screen composition focusing on material contrast.
- **Left Side (Devin)**: A single, perfectly smooth, high-polish black obsidian cube. It is solid, mysterious, and premium. It represents a "Black Box" proprietary system that works perfectly but is closed to inspection.
- **Right Side (OpenDevin)**: A complex, three-dimensional wireframe cube made of thin, glowing white light filaments. It occupies the same space but is completely transparent and visible, representing an open-source system that invites inspection and modification.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Philosophical and architectural
- Contrast between "Hidden Solidity" and "Visible Structure"
- Dark charcoal backgrounds on both sides
- Premium Materiality (Obsidian vs. Electric Light)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional dual-composition photography in a dark, minimalist studio. On the left half, a single large, high-polish black obsidian cube with sharp edges is perfectly centered. On the right half, an identical-sized 3D wireframe cube is made of thin, razor-sharp glowing white light filaments. Both cubes sit on a dark charcoal platform and are unified by the same deep shadows and cinematic side lighting. Deep bokeh on the background. No text, no artifacts, premium tech aesthetic. Shot on 50mm primary lens. 8k resolution, photorealistic obsidian and light textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Comparison**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Equality in Complexity, Difference in Transparency."

**Step 2: Refining the "Obsidian" cube**
If the black cube looks like plastic, guide Gemini:
> *“Enhance the reflections on the obsidian cube. It should look like heavy, volcanic glass—dark, deep, and slightly mirror-like. The reflections of the white wireframe cube from the other side should be subtly visible on its surface, showing they exist in the same environment.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the "Wireframe" precision**
If the wireframe looks like a simple 2D drawing, ask for 3D depth:
> *“The glowing white wireframe should have clear three-dimensional depth. Ensure we can see the 'internal' lines of the cube through the front layers. The points where the light lines intersect should be slightly brighter, representing 'Nodes' of development or information.”*
