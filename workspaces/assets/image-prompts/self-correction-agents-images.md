# Visual Strategy: Self-Correction Loops (The Refinement)

This guide shows how to create an "Iterative Perfection" visual representing self-correction and quality refinement using minimalist optical and mirror themes.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Becoming Better"
**Concept**: A professional studio photograph of a single glowing white sphere (The Draft) placed between two highly polished parallel mirrors. The camera captures the infinite reflections of the sphere. The reflections in the distance are sharper and clearer than the one in the foreground, symbolizing the AI refining and perfecting its answer through self-correction.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Mathematical and infinite
- Clean, symmetrical, and philosophical
- Stark and minimalist
- High-end material luxury (Mirror-finish glass, pure light)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Professional editorial photography in a dark, minimalist studio. A single, glowing pearl-white sphere is placed between two perfectly parallel, high-polish black mirrors. The photograph captures the infinite recursive reflections of the sphere disappearing into the distance. The reflections are perfectly aligned and become progressively sharper and more luminous. Cinematic lighting, deep shadows, premium tech aesthetic. Sharp focus on the first three reflections. No text, no artifacts, minimalist composition. Shot on 50mm primary lens. 8k resolution, photorealistic mirror and porcelain textures."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Reflection**
Submit the initial prompt. You are looking for a sense of "Infinite Iteration."

**Step 2: Refining the "Quality"**
If the reflections look messy or distorted, guide Gemini:
> *“Ensure the mirrors are perfectly parallel so the reflections form a straight, clean line. The spheres should look like they are being edited—maybe the first sphere has a slight grain, and each subsequent reflection becomes smoother and more perfectly formed.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Light**
If the scene is too bright, ask for more drama:
> *“Keep the environment very dark. The only source of light should be the glowing spheres themselves. The reflections should be the only visible things in the black abyss between the mirrors. This reinforces the idea of self-correction being a focused, internal process.”*
