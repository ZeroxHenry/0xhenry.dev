# Visual Strategy: Cross-Encoders (The Sieve)

This guide shows how to generate a visual representing "Advanced Filtering" that looks like high-end industrial design photography rather than a generic AI diagram.

---

### 1. Visual Intent: "Precision Selection"
**Concept**: A macro photograph of a precision metallic filter. A single, distinct, glowing golden particle is perfectly aligned with a mesh opening, while other dull grey particles are resting on top. This symbolizes the re-ranker choosing the perfect context from a set of candidates.

**Target Vibe**: 
- Macro industrial photography
- Precise and technical
- Selective focus (Depth of field)
- High contrast (Dark metal vs. Golden light)

---

### 2. Direct Gemini Prompt
> **Prompt**: "Macro extreme close-up of a high-precision, dark brushed steel micro-mesh filter. A single, small, glowing golden spherical light particle is positioned exactly in the center, passing through one of the precise geometric holes of the mesh. Several other dull, dark grey matte spheres are scattered on the surface but are not passing through. Deep bokeh and cinematic shallow depth of field, sharp focus only on the golden point. Soft dramatic side lighting, realistic metallic textures, professional product photography style. Shot on 100mm macro lens. 8k resolution, photorealistic."

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Gemini Interaction Guide

**Step 1: The First Capture**
Submit the prompt. You are looking for those "tangible" textures of brushed steel and the clean glow of the golden particle.

**Step 2: Refining Textures**
If the metal looks too smooth or "plastic," tell Gemini:
> *“Keep the composition but enhance the texture of the metal. I want to see fine industrial brushing marks on the steel and subtle imperfections that make it look real. Make the golden glow feel like a focused point of energy, not a flat yellow circle.”*

**Step 3: Controlling the Blur**
If the background is too busy, ask for more focus:
> *“Increase the depth of field blur (bokeh) so that only the central golden particle and its immediate surroundings are sharp. Everything else should fade into a dark, creamy background.”*
