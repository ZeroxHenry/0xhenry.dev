### Introduction

For the past two years, we have treated LLMs as advanced encyclopedias. We ask a question, and it gives an answer. This is a **Linear Workflow**. But in 2026, the paradigm is shifting toward **AI Agents**.

An agent doesn't just answer; it **Acts**. 

If you ask a standard LLM to "Plan a vacation," it gives you a list of cities. If you ask an **AI Agent** to "Plan a vacation," it searches for flights, checks your calendar for conflicts, compares hotel prices, and proposes a finalized itinerary with booking links.

---

### The Four Pillars of an AI Agent

A true AI agent is defined by four capabilities:

1.  **Reasoning**: The ability to break a complex goal into a sequence of smaller steps (Planning).
2.  **Memory**: The ability to remember past actions and learn from mistakes (Short-term/Long-term memory).
3.  **Tool Use**: The ability to call external APIs (Search, Calculator, Code Interpreter).
4.  **Autonomy**: The ability to decide which tool to use and when to stop without human intervention.

---

### Why "Agentic Workflows" over "Smart Models"?

Andrew Ng famously argued that **Agentic Workflows** using a smaller, faster model (like Gemma 2 or GPT-4o-mini) can often outperform a single massive model (like GPT-4o) used in a linear way. 

This is because an agentic workflow is **Iterative**. It can draft an answer, check it for errors, refine it, and then present the final result. It's the difference between a student taking a test in one go vs. a student who drafts, reviews, and edits their essay.

---

### The Paradigm Shift: From Chat to Task

We are moving away from the "Chat Box" and toward "Task Completion."
-   **Old Way**: "Write an email to my boss about the budget."
-   **New Agentic Way**: "Manager, research the Q3 budget discrepancy and email the stakeholders with a summary and a meeting invite."

In the agentic way, the AI is no longer a tool you hold; it's a collaborator you manage.

---

### Summary

AI Agents represents the transformation of LLMs from "static knowledge" to "active intelligence." By giving AI the ability to reason, remember, and use tools, we are unlocking automation that was previously impossible.
