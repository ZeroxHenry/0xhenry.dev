From my experience with numerous job changes and hires, I have one conviction: a well-managed **tech blog** is more powerful than any flashy portfolio. Why? Is it just because there are more records? No. It’s because a blog broadcasts your **'process of thinking'** in real-time.

---

### 1. The Value of 'Trial and Error' over the Final Product

A portfolio only shows the result. It says, "I finished this project." But a tech blog shows the **history**: "I struggled with a `Memory Leak` for 3 days and finally solved it by forming this hypothesis." Recruiters want to know your attitude when facing problems more than the result itself.

---

### 2. Proof of Communication Skills

Development is ultimately teamwork. Being able to break down and explain complex technical concepts in writing so that colleagues can understand proves that you will be a great communicator within the team. The blog posts themselves become a portfolio of your 'documentation skills.'

---

### 3. Proof of Sustainability

The fact that you've run a blog for over a year is the surest evidence of your **'consistency'** and **'passion for technology.'** A forced portfolio is quickly exposed, but a blog with dozens of entries stands for solid skills built over that period.

---

### Henry's Tip: "Be Honest Rather Than Perfect"

Don't just try to write perfect answers on your blog. Instead, posts that start with **"What I didn't know"** resonate more. Record your growing pains. Those records gathered together will be the most powerful certificate of who you are.
