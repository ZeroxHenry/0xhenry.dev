The biggest fear for junior developers is, "What if I don't improve because of AI?" Indeed, if you thoughtlessly copy and paste code AI writes for you, your skills will likely be at a standstill three years from now.

However, if you know how to **'dominate'** AI, you can absorb the knowledge that took seniors 10 years to accumulate in just two. Here is my AI learning recipe for juniors.

---

### 1. Asking 'Why' (Reverse Questioning)

When AI writes code for you, don't just hit the run button. Get into the habit of asking:
> "Why did you use `forEach` instead of `map` here?"
> "What are the performance benefits?"
> "Are there better alternatives?"
The process of critically accepting AI's answers is the best form of study.

---

### 2. Utilizing Mock Code Reviews

Give code you wrote yourself to an AI and say, "Review this like a ruthless senior developer." It will point out numerous things you missed in terms of edge cases, readability, and memory efficiency. This is often faster and more detailed than actual reviews you receive at work.

---

### 3. Having 'No-AI' Focus Time

You must set aside a few hours a week to solve complex logic using only official documentation and your own head, with both the internet and AI turned off. It’s like lifting heavy weights to build muscle. AI is just assistive equipment; your core strength comes from your own brain.

---

### Henry's Advice: "There is Poison in Comfort"

AI makes you comfortable, but comfort is the enemy of growth. Never stop the 'uncomfortable' process of thinking, "How can I understand this more deeply?" Make AI your **'brain that expands thought,'** not just your 'hands.'
