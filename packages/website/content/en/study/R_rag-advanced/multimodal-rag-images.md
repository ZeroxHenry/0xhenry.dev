### Introduction

We’ve already learned about Text RAG—retrieving documents to answer questions. But what if your "knowledge base" consists of photos, architecture diagrams, or medical scans? To handle this, we need **Multimodal RAG**.

Multimodal RAG allows a user to type "Show me the blue blueprints for the living room" and have the system retrieve the exact image, even if the image has no tags or text descriptions attached to it.

---

### The Multimodal RAG Architecture

The workflow is similar to Text RAG, but with a visual twist:

1.  **Ingestion (Indexing)**:
    -   Take all your images.
    -   Pass them through a **Vision Encoder** (like CLIP or SigLIP).
    -   Store the resulting vectors in a **Vector Database** (Milvus, Pinecone, or Chroma).
2.  **Retrieval**:
    -   The user enters a text query: "Sunset over a lake."
    -   Convert that text into a vector using the **CLIP Text Encoder**.
    -   Perform a vector similarity search in the DB to find the closest image vectors.
3.  **Generation (Optional)**:
    -   Pass the retrieved image and the user's question to a **Vision-LLM** (like LLaVA).
    -   The AI explains *why* this image matches the query or answers specific questions about its content.

---

### Why this is superior to traditional tagging

-   **Infinite Flexibility**: You don't need to pre-tag images. You can search for things you never thought of before, like "patterns that look like a spiderweb."
-   **Semantic Understanding**: The system understands "vibe" and "composition," not just keywords.
-   **Scale**: You can index millions of images automatically in a few hours.

---

### Implementation Tip: Dual Embeddings

For the most robust systems, we often use **Dual Embeddings**:
-   **Vector 1**: The CLIP image embedding (for visual similarity).
-   **Vector 2**: A text embedding of the image's *caption* (generated by a model like BLIP-2).

By searching across both, you ensure that the system understands both the visual pixels and the high-level semantic meaning of the image.

---

### Summary

Multimodal RAG turns a "dump of images" into a searchable, intelligent library. It is the final piece of the puzzle for building AI systems that can truly work with the messy, visual world we live in.
