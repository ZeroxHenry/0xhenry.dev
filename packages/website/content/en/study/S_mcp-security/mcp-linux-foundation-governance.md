A monumental event recently occurred in the AI industry: **MCP (Model Context Protocol)**, ambitiously launched by Anthropic, has been transferred to the **Linux Foundation** as an official project.

This isn't just a change of ownership. It symbolizes the shift of the AI agent ecosystem from 'Corporate Proprietary' to **'Public Standard.'**

---

### 1. Why the Linux Foundation?

The Linux Foundation has neutrally managed the foundations of modern IT, from the Linux Kernel to Kubernetes and Node.js. MCP joining this ranks means it will no longer be an Anthropic-only technology, but a **Neutral Standard** followed by OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and all other major LLM providers.

---

### 2. The Shift to Standardization: The 'USB' of AI

We don't care about the computer manufacturer when we buy a mouse. That's because of the USB standard.

- **Before Standardization**: You had to develop separate tools for OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.
- **After Standardization**: Develop one MCP-compliant tool, and it works flawlessly with any model.

This heralds an explosion in the Tool Market.

---

### 3. What It Means for Developers: A Mandatory Skill

MCP is no longer just a 'nice-to-have' tech; it's a **Mandatory Protocol** for AI engineers. Just as web devs must know HTTP, agent devs must master connecting data and tools via MCP.

The 'first-mover advantage' Anthropic had is gone, but the pie has grown much larger for everyone.

---

### 4. Henry's Outlook: The Birth of an "Agent OS"

MCP will eventually act as the **Operating System (OS)** for AI agents. Everything—file systems, databases, web searching, emails—will be unified under the language of MCP. Just as Linux conquered the server world, MCP is prepared to conquer the AI agent world.

---

### Conclusion

The move of MCP to the Linux Foundation is the democratization and industrialization of AI. Welcome the emergence of a non-proprietary standard, and remember: you now have the strongest justification ever to adopt MCP in your next project.
