In 2026, the question "Can you build a bulletin board with JavaScript?" has become meaningless. If you give an AI agent 10 seconds, it will produce more perfect code than you. Does this mean we developers are now obsolete?

The short answer is: **"AI is just a tool; defining the purpose remains a human role."** I’m sharing three new survival keywords for the AI agent era that are more important than sheer coding skill.

---

### 1. From Coder to Architect

The ability to **design how the entire system meshes together** has become far more important than the ability to craft individual functions precisely. We must evolve into 'orchestrators' who define interfaces and coordinate data flows so that agents can perform their assigned tasks effectively.

---

### 2. The Power of Inquiry: Problem Definition

AI is genius at providing answers but terrible at asking questions. Asking **fundamental questions** like "Is this feature truly necessary for the user?" or "Will this architecture be maintainable in a year?" becomes a developer's core competitive edge. Good questions make for good code.

---

### 3. Debugging Sensitivity

AI-generated code works 90% of the time, but the remaining 10% of errors are much more subtle and difficult for humans to find. The **'technical intuition'** to read the nuances of code and deduce why a model produced a specific bug only comes from years of hands-on experience. Being able to audit the code AI writes is key to survival.

---

### Henry's Take: "Ride the Technical Wave"

If you try to swim against a wave, you'll be swept away, but if you get on a surfboard, you'll move faster than anyone. Don't fear the massive wave of AI agents; be the surfer who carries your design skills and business insights to create greater value.
